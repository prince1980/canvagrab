Why Canva Videos Are Hard to Download

Canva portfolio pages and Canva-published websites deliver videos using adaptive streaming — specifically the DASH (Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP) protocol. This means:

  • Video and audio are served as separate streams (usually .mp4 and .m4a files)
  • Standard right-click > "Save video" only captures the video track — no audio
  • Browser developer tools show dozens of fragmented network requests, making manual downloading impractical
  • YouTube downloaders and generic video scrapers don't understand Canva's URL structure

CanvaGrab solves all of this by running a real headless browser, intercepting every media network request, then using FFmpeg to merge video and audio into a single complete MP4 — the same way a professional video editor would.
